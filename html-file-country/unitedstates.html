  • Launch Date: 10 August 1966

    Purpose: Lunar Orbiter
    Weight: 385.6 kg
    Power: -



    The Lunar Orbiter 1 robotic spacecraft, part of NASA's Lunar Orbiter program, was the first American spacecraft to orbit the Moon. It was designed primarily to photograph smooth areas of the lunar surface for selection and verification of safe landing sites for the Surveyor and Apollo missions. It was also equipped to collect selenodetic, radiation intensity, and micrometeoroid impact data.

  • Launch Date: 30 May 1966
    Operational Till: 7 January 1967
    Purpose: Lunar Lander
    Weight: 292 kg
    Power: -



    Surveyor 1 was the first lunar soft-lander in the uncrewed Surveyor program of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA, United States). This lunar soft-lander gathered data about the lunar surface that would be needed for the crewed Apollo Moon landings that began in 1969. The successful soft landing of Surveyor 1 on the Ocean of Storms was the first by an American space probe on any extraterrestrial body,[2] occurring on the first attempt and just four months after the first Moon landing by the Soviet Union's Luna 9 probe.
